Program service expenses/Total expenses: What percentage of the charity’s spending is on actual charity? A healthy charity will spend atleast 75% on their cause.
Management and general expenses/Total expenses: What percentage of the charity’s spending is on administrative costs?
Fundraising expenses/Total expenses: What percentage of the charity’s spending is on fundraising?
Net income margin (Total Revenue – Total Expenses)/(Total Revenue): How much of the charity’s annual revenue is being retained by the NPO? While for-profit companies would like this ratio to be as high as possible, a charity should be spending their revenue on their cause and thus their net income should be near or below 0%.
Cash Buffer/Total expenses: How large is the cash buffer relative to total expenses? While a charity should have a cash buffer to guard against potential fluctuations in donations, an excessive amount of spare cash is somewhat of a red flag. It could mean that the charity has run out of charitable things to spend it on, meaning your future donations to the charity might sit idly doing nothing
